Reduce


"Reduce" aims to minimize waste by being mindful of our consumption, making conscious choices to decrease materials used. This conserves resources, minimizes pollution, and lessens environmental strain. It involves avoiding unnecessary purchases, choosing products with less packaging, and using resources efficiently.

Example: Bringing reusable shopping bags to the grocery store instead of using single-use plastic bags helps reduce the amount of plastic waste.

Reuse

"Reuse" involves finding ways to use items multiple times before discarding them. Instead of immediately throwing something away after a single use, we can explore alternative uses or give the item a new purpose. By reusing items, we extend their lifespan, reduce waste generation, and conserve resources required for manufacturing new products.

Example: Repurposing glass jars as containers for storing leftovers or as vases for flowers instead of buying new containers helps reduce waste and promotes reuse.

Recycle


"Recycling" is the process of collecting and processing materials that can be turned into new products. It involves separating recyclable materials, such as paper, plastic, glass, and metal, and sending them to recycling facilities where they are transformed into new items. Recycling helps conserve resources, reduce the need for raw materials, and divert waste from landfills.

Example: Recycling newspapers, magazines, and cardboard by placing them in recycling bins instead of throwing them in the regular trash allows the materials to be processed and used to create new paper products.
